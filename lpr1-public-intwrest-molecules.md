# Legislative Proposal Record (LPR) – Standard Format
**1. Metadata (Header)**
 * **Title:** EU PIM Infrastructure Regulation (Public Interest Molecules)
 * **Date:** April 24, 2026
 * **Submitting Entity (Author):** Gemini User Discourse / Strategic Design Module
 * **AI Assistance Declaration:** Gemini 3.1 Pro (Paid Tier)
 * **Legal Level:** EU Regulation (directly applicable)
 * **Jurisdiction:** Entire European Union (EU Single Market)
**2. Problem Statement (Context & Motivation)**
 * **Status Quo:** Highly effective, unpatentable substances (e.g., endogenous peptides) fail to achieve clinical approval because the current system requires exclusive composition of matter patents as the refinancing engine for Phase III trials.
 * **Deficit Analysis:** Market failure regarding "Public Interest Molecules". Private investors avoid unpatentable active ingredients; state-funded research ends in the "Valley of Death" prior to clinical Phase III due to insurmountable capital barriers and bureaucratic complexity.
 * **Requirement:** Creation of a capital-light, evidence-based approval and production pathway under state auspices, featuring a multi-layered security architecture for the preventive mitigation of clinical, financial, and technical risks.
**3. The Proposal (Proposed Decision)**
 * **Conceptual Logic:** Transformation of drug approval from a capital-intensive betting system into a data-driven infrastructure service ("Open Source Pharma").
 * **Mechanics:**
   1. **Safety Pre-Decision:** Proof of absence of toxicity (Phase I/II) funded through public research grants.
   2. **Phased RWE Approval (Phased Rollout):** Granting of a conditional market approval. Stage 1 is strictly limited to certified *Centers of Excellence* (e.g., university hospitals). Automatic release for the outpatient sector only occurs upon reaching defined statistical confidence intervals (e.g., 10,000 unremarkable patient months).
   3. **Clinical Risk Reduction (Kill Switches & Stratification):** Prescriptions are obligatorily linked to companion diagnostics (biomarkers). An algorithmic kill switch monitors adverse event rates via the European Health Data Space (EHDS); if dynamic thresholds are exceeded, an EU-wide, automatic API lock for new prescriptions is triggered.
   4. **State-as-a-Service Production (Operational Safeguard):** Decentralized Container-GMP replaces monolithic mega-factories. Identical, standardized micro-facilities are redundantly distributed across the EU. A dual-sourcing strategy integrates private Contract Manufacturing Organizations (CMOs) via cost-plus contracts as a silent reserve.
   5. **Execution Interface (Middleware Concept & Decoupling):** To avoid complex retrofitting of national systems, a central **PIM API Gateway (Abstraction Layer)** is established. National telematics infrastructures merely forward "PIM"-flagged prescriptions as standardized, encrypted data blobs using **HL7 FHIR** to this gateway.
   6. **Isolated Logic:** The entire processing logic—validating zero-knowledge proofs for pharmacies, synchronizing with kill switches, and federated learning routines—takes place exclusively within the European middleware. National systems do not require access to the underlying PIM logic.
   7. **Liability Chain & EU Budget Protection:** Strict product liability transfers to the "EU Public Pharma Trust," indemnifying the researchers. A PIM risk premium (micro-tax) is levied to recapitalize the fund. Patients sign an opt-in contract ("therapy under experimental conditions"). Extreme "black swan" toxicities are hedged via pharma catastrophe bonds (cat bonds) on the capital market.
**4. Regulatory Hierarchy & Interdependencies (Dependencies)**
 * **Vertical Dependencies:** Harmonization with EU Directive 2001/83/EC (Community code relating to medicinal products) and Regulation (EC) No 726/2004 (Centralised procedure).
 * **Horizontal Dependencies:** Integration into the *European Health Data Space (EHDS)*. The middleware model relies on HL7 FHIR as a bridging technology to national systems.
 * **Conflict Potential:** Potential legal challenges regarding the lowering of strict liability standards via the patient opt-in contract.
**5. Impact Assessment**
 * **Primary Effects:** Safe, continuously monitored scaling of innovative therapies coupled with a drastic financial unburdening of solidarity-based health systems.
 * **Secondary Effects:** Establishment of Europe as the global pioneer for decentralized crypto-medical data (zero-knowledge proofs) and modular high-tech pharmaceutical manufacturing.
 * **Political Leverage:** The implementation barrier for member states is drastically lowered. National health ministries merely need to implement a dedicated routing command for PIM preparations without fundamentally altering their core IT architectures.
 * **Budgetary Impact:** Initial CapEx for the decentralized container GMP network and the central API gateway. Ongoing operations are refinanced through at-cost dispensing, the micro-tax, and cat bonds.
 * **Subsidiarity Check:** Necessary at the EU level, as the Real-World Evidence (RWE) procedure and the central gateway require the data scaling and financial backing of the entire single market.
**6. Evaluated & Rejected Alternatives**
 * **Native System Overhaul:** Rejected in favor of the middleware concept, as demanding profound structural changes to 27 fragmented national IT infrastructures would be politically and technically doomed to fail.
 * **Centralized Data Storage (Data Lake):** Rejected in favor of Federated Learning and Zero-Knowledge Proofs to ensure strict compliance with GDPR and maintain the highest standards of medical confidentiality.
**7. Unresolved Issues & Consultation Needs**
 * **Expert Check:** Precise definition of the HL7 FHIR specifications for the PIM data blob to guarantee flawless cross-border interoperability.
 * **Discussion Points:** Parameterization of the dynamic thresholds for the "API Kill Switch" within the middleware to effectively prevent false alarms (false positives) that could prematurely derail a successful RWE trial.
