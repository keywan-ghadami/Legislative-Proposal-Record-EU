# Legislative Proposal Records (LPR) – European Union Strategic Design Hub

This repository serves as a decentralized incubator for highly structured, technocratic legislative drafts specifically tailored to the **European Union (EU)** and the European Single Market. It is designed to shift European political discourse from fragmented, lobby-driven rhetoric toward data-driven, infrastructural legislative design.

## 1. Vision & Purpose (EU Focus)

Navigating the legislative machinery of the EU—balancing supranational regulations with 27 distinct national infrastructures—requires more than traditional policy papers. The **Legislative Proposal Record (LPR)** format was developed to address the unique complexities of the European ecosystem:

* **Enforce Precision:** Replace vague declarations of intent with rigorous operational logic suitable for EU Regulations and Directives.
* **Ensure European Interoperability:** Treat legislation as a cross-border "system update" that must account for existing EU frameworks (e.g., GDPR, EHDS) and technical interfaces (e.g., HL7 FHIR).
* **Objectify Discourse:** Handle proposals as "Living Documents" refined through peer review, enabling a unified technical standard before entering formal trilogue negotiations or European Commission reviews.

## 2. The LPR Specification (Version 1.0)

Every entry in this repository must follow a strict 7-point modular structure to ensure maximum comparability and analytical depth across member states:

1.  **Metadata (Header):** Unique identification, authorship (including AI assistance declarations), legal level (e.g., EU Regulation, Directive), and jurisdiction (Entire EU or specific mechanisms).
2.  **Problem Statement (Context & Motivation):** Analysis of the status quo within the EU, deficit analysis (market/system failure), and explicit requirements for the solution.
3.  **The Proposal (Proposed Decision):** The core logic. Description of mechanics, operational interfaces (middleware concepts), and security architecture (e.g., algorithmic kill switches, stratification).
4.  **Regulatory Hierarchy & Interdependencies:** Integration into existing EU law (e.g., Directive 2001/83/EC) and technical dependencies across national healthcare or IT systems.
5.  **Impact Assessment:** Primary and secondary effects, European budgetary impact, and mandatory subsidiarity checks (justifying action at the EU level rather than the national level).
6.  **Evaluated & Rejected Alternatives:** Documentation of discarded approaches to prevent repetitive debates ("Why not mandate a native system overhaul for all 27 member states?").
7.  **Unresolved Issues & Consultation Needs:** Explicit identification of technical, statistical, or legal parameters requiring further definition by European expert committees (e.g., EMA, eHealth Network).

## 3. Collaboration & Governance

This repository is an active workspace for European policymakers, technical architects, and domain experts. We encourage constructive criticism and rigorous stress-testing of all proposals.

### Discussion via Pull Requests (PRs)
* **Refinement:** If you identify a logical gap or wish to specify technical parameters (e.g., tuning a threshold for EU-wide data sharing), please submit a PR.
* **Modularity:** PRs should ideally target specific sections of an LPR to maintain a clean version history and facilitate targeted debates.

### Submitting New LPRs
* Use the provided `LPR_Template.md`.
* Maintain the distinction between the political goal (*What?*) and the technical implementation (*How?*).
* Ensure the proposal reaches a level of complexity suitable for direct consideration by the European Parliament, the Council of the EU, or the European Commission.

### External Platforms & Committees
The LPR format is designed to be exported into formal EU political processes. Discussions may occur in GitHub Issues, but the records are also intended for use in expert councils, NGOs, and legislative drafting sessions in Brussels and Strasbourg. Please always reference the specific commit hash of the LPR being discussed.

## 4. AI-Human Collaboration Disclosure
Many records in this repository are generated or refined using high-level Large Language Models. This collaboration is explicitly declared in the metadata of each LPR to maintain transparency regarding the "Strategic Design Module" used in drafting EU policies.
